Following the Spanish Supreme Court´s ruling of 23rd of December 2015, the Courts of Justice declare as abusive mortgages those cases where the cost of formalizing them has been imposed, supporting thereby the customers´ rights to recover the expenses imposed unilaterally at the time of applying for mortgages, leaving them with no opportunity to negotiate the aforementioned expenditure clause.

This is because those expenses should have been paid by the Banks, since they were the ones interested in the registration of the mortgage with the Land Registry as a guarantee of the mortgage payments.

Therefore, if you have a loan mortgage with any Spanish bank, when signing your mortgage, the bank kept some money to pay on your behalf, the expenses of the registration of the mortgage loan deed and now, you can claim your bank and recover your money.

We advise you to claim your right to get your money back!


Who can claim?

Anyone who has signed a mortgage deed in Spain and was obliged by the bank to pay the expenses of said deed.


What expenses can you claim from your bank?

Corresponding costs from the Notary, Property or Land Registry, appraisal of the property, management expenses and the amount paid for the concept of Tax of Documented Legal Acts (Actos Jurídicos Documentados AJD). All this amounts to approximately 3.000€ that can be recovered, plus the legal interest and court costs, for a mortgage of 200.000 €.


What do you need in order to claim the unduly paid expenses from your bank?

In order to make a claim, you have to have:

  • A copy of your mortgage deed.
  • A copy of the invoices paid for the mortgage to the Notary, Land Registry, Registration proceedings, etc.
  • A copy of the AJD tax paid (tax form 600).
  • A copy of the invoice showing the amount paid to the bank agency to manage the registration process of the mortgage at the Land Registry.

In case you do not have them, we will claim them on your behalf from your bank.


What are the first steps to be taken?

We will claim directly the payment of the mortgage expenses to the Customer Service Department of the Bank with whom the mortgage loan was contracted.

After two months from the complaint, whether or not the Customer Service Department has replied, or if the response has been negative, we will proceed to take the corresponding legal action.

What must be requested in the lawsuit brought before the Court, is the nullity of the abusive clauses of the mortgage and the restitution of the expenses paid as a result of that agreement.


How long does this procedure normally take?

Being only a documentary proof, the trial phase is avoided, therefore the procedure is much shorter than normal.


Is there a deadline to sue your bank?

No. The nullity action is indefeasible and the reimbursement obligation is complementary to said nullity, so there is no deadline indicated by law. Even if your mortgage has already been cancelled, you will still have the right to claim what you paid for the mortgage expenses from your bank.


Where do we make the demand?

At the Court where you have your domicile, which enables you to claim what your bank owes you with no obstacles.
